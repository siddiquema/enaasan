Am I Ready for a Communication-Heavy Role?

A 30-item self-assessment across six communication areas. Scoring guide, role-readiness interpretation, and a 3-month development plan for gaps. Know your communication strengths and gaps honestly.

✅ Self-Assessment & Development

What This Assessment Does

This 30-item checklist helps you honestly assess whether you're ready for roles that demand strong communication skills (customer-facing, team leadership, sales) — and identify gaps before applying.

Be honest. This checklist is for you, not for a recruiter. Accurate self-assessment is the only path to real improvement.

Scoring & Interpretation

What Your Total Score Means
24–30 out of 30
Strong Communicator
You're ready for communication-heavy roles now. Apply for BPS voice, customer-facing, or junior management roles. Use your strengths as a talking point in interviews.
16–23 out of 30
Solid Foundation with Gaps
You have the basics. Identify your 2–3 weakest sections and work on them for 2–3 months while applying. Mention what you're developing in interviews ("I'm actively working on my persuasion skills...").
8–15 out of 30
Inconsistent Communicator
You have potential but need deliberate development. Consider starting in non-customer-facing roles (back office, data processing) while you build, or invest 3–6 months in focused practice first.
0–7 out of 30
Significant Gaps
Communication-heavy roles will be challenging right now. Start in execution roles and build from there. The development plan below is your map for the next 3–4 months.

If You Have Gaps — Development Plan

A 3–4 Month Focused Practice Plan
Month 1–2: Self-Awareness

• Ask trusted people: "What's one thing I could improve about how I communicate?"

• Record yourself (video call, voice note) and listen/watch. How do you actually come across?

• Re-read the Framework card; identify your 2–3 weakest competencies

Month 2–3: Deliberate Practice

• For listening: Assign yourself "no interrupting" in one meeting/week. Notice the discipline it takes.

• For clarity: Write an email, then cut 30% of words. Practice this weekly.

• For EI: In one difficult conversation/week, name what you observe: "I notice you seem frustrated..."

• For persuasion: Propose one idea in a meeting. Prepare data/logic first, not just opinions.

• For adaptability: Have a conversation with someone very different from you; adjust your style intentionally.

Month 3–4: Feedback Loop

• Find a mentor or trusted friend who will give you honest feedback

• Ask them: "How did that come across?" after important conversations

• Adjust based on feedback and repeat

Next Steps

Take this assessment seriously. Your honest score is your baseline. Use the development plan to focus your practice on your weakest 2 competencies. Most people improve measurably in 3–4 months with deliberate effort.

After the readiness card, the final guide shows you concrete examples of how communication quality compounds over a 20-year career. That's your motivator to keep practicing.
