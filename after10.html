After Class 10

Want to start earning early? That's a strategy, not a setback.

Real paths that get you skilled and earning within months to two years — every one of them with a route back to higher education if and when you want it. The door never closes.

Looking for state-specific exams (TN, Karnataka, Kerala, AP, Telangana)? →

Read this first. Choosing to earn after Class 10 does not mean you are giving up on your future. Some of India's most secure and well-paid careers — in railways, defence, skilled trades, and technical fields — start exactly here. What matters is choosing a path with growth built in, not just the first job you find. That's what this page shows you.

Path 1 — Skill up, earn fast

Trades and training that pay within 6–24 months

These are structured, government-recognised routes — not dead-end jobs. Each one leads to a recognised certificate that employers across India (and abroad) understand.

Skill tradeEarning in 1–2 yrs
ITI — Industrial Training Institute
Electrician, Fitter, Welder, COPA, Mechanic & 100+ trades
Government-run skill training. Low fees (often under ₹5,000/year in govt ITIs), high employability in trades India is short of.
₹12–25k/month starting Details →
Duration1–2 years depending on trade
CostGovt ITIs: ₹1,000–5,000/year. Many states waive fees for SC/ST/BC students. Free hostels in many govt ITIs.
Best tradesElectrician, Fitter, and Welder have the strongest job demand. COPA (Computer Operator) suits those who want office/IT-side work.
After ITIApprenticeship (paid), private jobs, government technician posts, Gulf jobs (electricians/welders earn ₹60k–1.2L/month abroad), or your own shop.
↩ Back to studyITI + 2-year trade = Class 12 equivalent in most states. Direct entry to Polytechnic 2nd year. From there, B.Tech lateral entry is possible.
A govt ITI seat costs less than most coaching classes and leads to a real wage faster than almost any other option. But choose the trade by job demand, not by which has empty seats.
Official website ↗
Paid trainingEarning from day 1
Apprenticeship (NAPS)
National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme
Train inside a real company and get paid a monthly stipend while you learn. India's most underused opportunity.
₹5–9k/month stipend during training Details →
Duration6 months – 3 years depending on trade
Who can applyClass 10 pass (some trades need ITI first). Register free on the apprenticeship portal.
What you getMonthly stipend + real work experience + a government-recognised certificate (NCVT). Many companies absorb good apprentices into permanent roles.
↩ Back to studyApprenticeship certificate + experience counts toward lateral entry in many diploma programmes. You can also do NIOS Class 12 alongside.
Companies like Maruti, Tata Motors, L&T, and Indian Railways take thousands of apprentices every year. The stipend is modest, but you are being paid to become employable — the opposite of paying for a certificate.
Official website ↗
Short courseEarning in 3–12 months
PMKVY / Skill India courses
Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana — free short-term training
Free 3–12 month certified courses in retail, healthcare support, electronics repair, tailoring, beauty, logistics, and more.
₹10–18k/month starting Details →
Duration3–12 months, completely free at approved centres
Good optionsMobile/electronics repair, medical equipment assistant, logistics/warehouse operations, solar panel technician — sectors that are hiring.
Watch outQuality varies by training centre. Ask the centre for their placement record before joining — a genuine centre will show you.
↩ Back to studyNSQF certificates stack — you can climb levels (each level is a recognised qualification) and bridge into ITI or diploma programmes.
Best used as a fast entry into work, not a final destination. Combine a PMKVY certificate with NIOS Class 12 (below) and you keep every future option open.
Official website ↗
DiplomaEarning in 3 yrs
Polytechnic Diploma
3-year engineering diploma after Class 10 (POLYCET in TS/AP)
A recognised engineering qualification 2 years faster than the 12th + B.Tech route. Junior engineer roles in govt and private sector.
₹15–30k/month starting Details →
Duration3 years after Class 10
EntryPOLYCET entrance in TS/AP (much easier than JEE/EAMCET). Govt polytechnic fees are low; fee reimbursement schemes apply in TS/AP.
Leads toJunior Engineer posts (Railways, electricity boards, PWD), private sector technician/supervisor roles, or further study.
↩ Back to studyDiploma holders enter B.Tech directly in 2nd year via lateral entry (ECET in TS/AP). Many working diploma holders finish B.Tech through evening colleges.
The diploma route to engineer is the classic "earn early, study later" path — you can be earning at 19 while your peers are still in college, then finish B.Tech evenings paid for by your own salary.
Official website ↗

Path 2 — Government jobs open at Class 10

Secure jobs you can apply for right now (or at 17–18)

These are permanent or long-term government positions where Class 10 is the qualifying education. Preparation can be done free with online materials — coaching is optional, not required.

Central govtApply at 18
Railways Group D
RRB Level-1 posts — track maintainer, helper, assistant
India's largest employer. Permanent central government job with pension contributions, medical cover, and quarters.
₹18k/month + allowances (Level 1) Details →
EligibilityClass 10 pass OR ITI. Age 18–33 (relaxations for reserved categories).
SelectionComputer-based test (maths, reasoning, general science, current affairs) + physical efficiency test + medical.
GrowthDepartmental exams promote Group D staff to technician and supervisor grades. Many senior railway staff started at Level 1.
↩ Back to studyRailways encourages further education — employees complete Class 12/degrees via distance mode and rise through internal exams.
Competition is intense (lakhs of applicants) — treat the exam like a 6–12 month project. Free prep material and previous papers are on the RRB site; a coaching class is not mandatory.
Official website ↗
DefenceApply at 17.5
Agniveer (Army / Navy / Air Force)
Agnipath scheme — soldier entry, some trades at Class 10
4-year service with strong pay, then a ₹11.7 lakh exit fund — or selection into permanent cadre for the top 25%.
₹30–40k/month during service Details →
EligibilityClass 10 for General Duty; Class 12 for technical trades. Age 17.5–21. Physical fitness standards apply.
What you get₹30–40k/month (rising yearly), food/lodging covered, ₹11.71 lakh Seva Nidhi fund on exit, skill certificates.
After 4 yearsTop 25% retained permanently. Others exit with the fund + priority in CAPF/police recruitment + credit toward civilian qualifications.
↩ Back to studyIn-service courses count toward Class 12/diploma credit via NIOS and IGNOU partnerships. The exit fund can finance a full degree debt-free.
Go in with a plan for both outcomes — retention and exit. The exit fund plus four years of discipline and certificates is a genuinely strong platform at age 22, but only if you use the in-service education options.
Official website ↗
Central govtApply at 18
India Post GDS
Gramin Dak Sevak — branch postmaster, delivery agent
Selection is purely on Class 10 marks — no exam. One of the simplest government entries that exists.
₹10–14k/month + allowances Details →
EligibilityClass 10 with maths and English. Age 18–40. Local language knowledge required. Basic computer training certificate.
SelectionMerit list from Class 10 marks only. No written exam, no interview. Apply online when notifications open (2–3 times a year).
GrowthGDS staff can appear for departmental exams to become permanent Postman/Mail Guard and later Postal Assistant — a full central govt career track.
↩ Back to studyThe job's fixed hours leave room for distance education. Many GDS staff complete degrees while working and move up internally.
Starting pay is modest, but the entry barrier is the lowest in government service and the internal promotion ladder is real. Good Class 10 marks matter here — they are the entire selection.
Official website ↗
State govtApply at 18
Police Constable (state)
TS / AP police and most state forces — some posts at Class 10
Respected, permanent state government job. Requirements vary by state — TS/AP constable posts generally ask for Class 12, but several states and some categories accept Class 10.
₹20–30k/month starting Details →
EligibilityVaries by state and post. Check your state police recruitment board notification — if it asks Class 12, NIOS (below) gets you there while you work.
SelectionWritten test + physical measurement + physical efficiency (running, jumps) + medical. Physical prep matters as much as the written exam.
GrowthConstable → Head Constable → ASI → SI through departmental exams. Many SIs and Inspectors began as constables.
↩ Back to studyDepartmental promotion exams reward further education. Constables routinely complete degrees via distance mode to qualify for SI exams.
Start physical training early — most rejections happen at the physical stage, not the written one. Free running grounds beat paid coaching here.
Official website ↗

Path 3 — Earn and study at the same time

Keep your education moving while you work

Working after Class 10 does not mean stopping at Class 10. These routes let you finish Class 12 — and beyond — around a job.

Open schoolingStudy at your pace
NIOS — Open School Class 12
National Institute of Open Schooling
A government board (like CBSE) where you study from home and take exams when ready. Fully valid for college, government jobs, and entrance exams.
Fits around any job Details →
How it worksRegister once, study with free materials, appear for exams twice a year (April & October). Pass subjects one at a time if needed — credits carry over.
CostAround ₹2,000–3,000 total registration for 5 subjects. Materials are free to download.
Is it valid?Yes — NIOS is a national board recognised for CUET, NEET, JEE, government jobs, and university admission. Lakhs of students use it every year.
↩ OpensEvery door that a regular Class 12 opens: college via CUET, entrance exams, higher govt job categories.
NIOS + a job is the single most powerful combination on this page. Two years of income AND a Class 12 certificate — you lose nothing and gain both.
Official website ↗
Evening studyWork by day, class by evening
Evening & part-time programmes
Evening polytechnics, part-time ITI, weekend skilling
Several government polytechnics and ITIs run evening or part-time batches designed for people who work during the day.
Salary + qualification together Details →
WhereCheck government polytechnics in your district for part-time diploma batches; industrial areas often have evening ITI sections.
Who it suitsSomeone already working (shop, workshop, delivery, family business) who wants a formal qualification without quitting.
↩ Back to studyA part-time diploma has the same lateral-entry rights into B.Tech as a full-time one.
Slower than full-time study — a part-time diploma can take 3.5–4 years. But it is paid for by your own earnings and costs your family nothing. That trade-off is worth naming with pride.
Employer supportAsk before you join
Employer-sponsored upskilling
Companies that pay for your education
Larger employers — especially in manufacturing, retail chains, and logistics — increasingly fund NIOS, diplomas, and certifications for promising young staff.
Education funded by employer Details →
How to find itWhen applying anywhere, ask: "Does the company support further study or certification?" Organised employers (company-owned stores, factories, warehouses) are far more likely to say yes than informal ones.
ExamplesApprentice-to-employee pipelines at manufacturers, retail chains funding supervisor certifications, logistics firms sponsoring forklift/warehouse-management licences.
↩ Back to studyChoosing the right first employer can mean your Class 12 and diploma get funded — turning a job into a scholarship.
Between two job offers, the one with training support is usually worth more than the one with ₹1,000 extra salary. Think of it as part of your pay.

The comeback ladders

The door back to education — drawn out, step by step

Not theory. These are established, recognised ladders that thousands of students climb every year. Green steps are earning milestones.

🔧 The Technician-to-Engineer ladder
Class 10Age ~16 → ITI trade (2 yrs)Earning by 18 → Polytechnic lateral entry (2 yrs)Diploma by 20 → B.Tech lateral entry — 2nd year (ECET)Engineer by 23
Same destination as the Class 12 → JEE route — reached while earning for most of the journey.
🏛️ The Govt-job-and-grow ladder
Class 10Age ~16 → Govt job at 18 (Railways / GDS / Police)Permanent income → Distance degree while working (IGNOU / Ambedkar OU)Degree by ~24 → Departmental exams → higher gradesOfficer track
Government service rewards internal exams and further qualifications — the first job is the floor, not the ceiling.
📖 The Work-plus-NIOS ladder
Class 10Age ~16 → Job / PMKVY course + NIOS Class 12Earning + studying → Class 12 certificate (NIOS)By ~18–19 → CUET → college, or higher govt job categoriesAll doors open
The lowest-cost ladder on this page — roughly ₹3,000 total for NIOS, studied around any job.

Money help most students never hear about

Scholarships and schemes that reduce the pressure

Before deciding that further study is unaffordable, check these. Many families qualify without knowing.

01
National Scholarship Portal (NSP)
Central government scholarships for SC/ST/OBC/minority and low-income students — pre-matric and post-matric. One application covers many schemes. scholarships.gov.in ↗
02
TS / AP fee reimbursement
Both Telangana and Andhra Pradesh reimburse tuition fees for eligible students (income-based) in polytechnic, ITI, and degree courses. Ask the college office directly — they process it.
03
Free hostels and stipends at govt ITIs
Many government ITIs offer free or subsidised hostels, and several states pay monthly stipends to ITI students from low-income families. Ask when you apply — it is rarely advertised.
04
Labour welfare board scholarships
If a parent is a registered construction or unorganised-sector worker, state labour welfare boards give education grants to their children. Registration is free at the local labour office.
And if your family can support further study — that is also a strong path, and nothing on this page argues against it.
