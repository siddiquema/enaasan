# Brand Guidelines

## Mission

Give every Indian student — regardless of city, school quality, or family access to good counselling — a clear-eyed, honest understanding of their options at each stage of education and early career, so decisions get made with information instead of pressure, guesswork, or comparison.

## Vision

A student anywhere in India, in any language enaasan supports, can find a straight answer to "what are my real options here, and what does each one actually involve" — without a sales pitch, without a paywall, and without having to already know the right question to ask.

## Origin

Enaasan was founded by someone with years of experience in learning and development, having watched first-hand how knowledge gaps shape careers and how early decisions made without information create consequences that take years to undo. The name comes from Tamil: *en* (my) + *aasan* (teacher) — not a formal institution or coaching centre, but a personal guide: someone in your corner who explains things clearly and has no agenda. (See `about.html`'s origin section — this guideline describes the same story, doesn't replace it.)

## Core Values

1. **Honest over impressive.** enaasan would rather say "this depends, and here's what it depends on" than manufacture false certainty. No claims of guaranteed outcomes — see the recurring "nobody honest can promise that" framing already used in the 90-Day Plan.
2. **Free, genuinely.** No coaching upsell, no "premium version," no paywall on core guidance. Where enaasan asks for anything (an email, a WhatsApp number — see `docs/website-update-spec.md` §1), it's optional and never gates content.
3. **India-first, not India-translated.** Content is built around Indian exams, institutions, streams, and job market realities from the start — not written generically and localised after the fact.
4. **Transparent about how content is made.** Enaasan is upfront that AI assists research and drafting, and that a named human reviews, shapes, and takes responsibility for what's published (see `about.html`'s transparency section, and the `author` field requirement in `architecture/metadata-standard.md`).
5. **Respect the reader's real constraints.** Money, location, family expectations, and time are treated as legitimate inputs to a decision, not obstacles to be talked around.
6. **Decisions are recoverable.** Enaasan consistently frames choices as reversible and iterative ("very few career choices are permanent or unfixable") rather than as one irreversible fork in the road.

## Writing Principles

See `writing-style.md` for the detailed rules. In short: plain English, short sentences, explain jargon the moment it's used, no idioms that don't travel, always name the trade-off rather than only the upside.

## What enaasan is not

- Not a coaching institute, and not affiliated with one
- Not a source of real-time cut-offs, seat availability, or admission status — always points to official sources for anything time-sensitive
- Not paid to recommend any course, college, or platform
- Not going to pretend a decision is simple when it genuinely isn't
