# Tone of Voice

## Voice vs. tone

Voice is constant across everything enaasan writes. Tone shifts slightly by audience and context (a Parent-facing note is gentler in places than a Graduate-facing Workbook, without ever becoming a different voice).

## The enaasan voice, in five traits

1. **Direct.** Answers the question asked, in the first sentence or two, before adding nuance. Compare the FAQ's answer style: "Choose based on a combination of interest, strength, and the kind of future you're picturing — not the stream's reputation," not three paragraphs of throat-clearing before the actual answer.
2. **Calm.** Never manufactures urgency or anxiety to make a point land. Career and education decisions in India already come with enough real pressure — enaasan's job is to lower the temperature, not raise it.
3. **Respectful of the reader's intelligence.** Explains reasoning rather than issuing verdicts. "NEET requires a strong, balanced performance across Physics, Chemistry, and Biology" gives the reader the actual mechanism, not just a yes/no.
4. **Honest about uncertainty.** Says "this depends on X, Y, Z" when it genuinely does, rather than picking a confident-sounding answer for the sake of sounding authoritative. Never promises an outcome ("this guide does not guarantee a job").
5. **Warm without being saccharine.** Cares visibly about the reader's actual situation (constraints, family pressure, fear of choosing wrong) without resorting to motivational-poster language ("you've got this!", "follow your dreams!").

## Tone by audience

| Audience | Tone adjustment |
|---|---|
| Graduates / Schools | Peer-to-older-sibling: direct, practical, treats the reader as capable of handling honest information |
| Parents | Slightly more measured pace, explicitly acknowledges parental concern as legitimate before addressing it — see the existing "For Parents" FAQ answers and `guide.html`'s myths section |
| Careers (general) | Neutral, informational, slightly more structured/reference-like than the Graduates tone |
| Workplace | Practical and outcome-focused, closer to the Graduates tone but assumes some working-world context already |

## What to avoid

- **Hype.** No "game-changing," "revolutionary," "unlock your potential." If a claim sounds like an ad, rewrite it.
- **Fear-mongering.** Don't manufacture stakes ("if you don't decide now, you'll fall behind") to motivate action. Motivate through clarity, not fear.
- **False certainty.** Don't flatten a genuinely conditional answer ("it depends on your risk tolerance") into a false absolute for the sake of a punchier sentence.
- **Condescension.** Explaining a term is not the same as talking down to the reader. Contrast "obviously, everyone knows that CUET is..." (condescending) with "CUET, the Common University Entrance Test, is..." (respectful).
- **Corporate distance.** Enaasan speaks as an individual guide (the "en-aasan," personal teacher framing), not as an institution issuing a bulletin.

## A worked contrast

**Not enaasan's tone:** "Unlock unlimited career potential with our revolutionary 90-day transformation plan — guaranteed results!"

**Enaasan's tone:** "This plan will not guarantee you a job — nobody honest can promise that. What 90 days of real work gets you is clarity and evidence." (Verbatim from the existing 90-Day Career Clarity Plan — this is the standard, not an aspiration.)
