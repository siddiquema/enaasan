# Governance

Governance documents define **how Enaasan operates** — the policies, processes, and principles that guide decision-making, quality standards, and long-term sustainability.

## Purpose

Governance is distinct from the Knowledge Standard v1.0 (frozen). While the Knowledge Standard defines *what knowledge is* and *how it is structured*, governance defines:

- **Research and IP standards** — how we conduct research, verify evidence, respect intellectual property, and maintain original work
- **Editorial policies** — editorial calibration rules extracted from real content (the nine rules in `standards/editorial-calibration-baseline.md`)
- **Operational procedures** — documented in `ops/OPS-001-editorial-workflow.md`
- **Change management** — how we track changes to governance without modifying the frozen standard

## Key Documents

- **GOV-001: Research and Intellectual Property Policy** — How Enaasan conducts research, verifies sources, respects copyright, and maintains original voice
- **Governance Change Log** — Record of all governance updates, rationale, and dates

## Governance vs. Knowledge Standard

| Aspect | Knowledge Standard v1.0 | Governance |
|---|---|---|
| **Status** | Frozen; requires evidence to change | Evolves based on operational evidence |
| **Scope** | Object types, metadata, relationships, philosophy | Operations, research, IP, editorial rules |
| **Change trigger** | End of Sprint 1 + friction evidence | Operational problems; can be updated mid-sprint |
| **Impact** | Affects every Knowledge Card published | Affects how Enaasan operates; does not change the standard itself |

## How to Update Governance

1. **Identify a governance problem** — operational friction, policy gap, or compliance issue
2. **Propose a change** — document the problem, the solution, and the rationale
3. **Record in the change log** — version number, date, author, summary, rationale
4. **Implement** — update the relevant governance document
5. **No Knowledge Standard change required** — governance updates do not affect EOS-001–004 unless explicitly linked

Governance documents are tools for making Enaasan more effective, sustainable, and trustworthy. The Knowledge Standard is law; governance is how we live under it.
