# Build Your Career Using AI

## Guide 1

### Use AI to Discover Careers That Suit You

## Guide Objective

By the end of this guide, students should have:

- A shortlist of 3-5 careers worth exploring.
- A better understanding of their own interests.
- An understanding of what those careers involve.
- A Career Exploration Sheet that they can use in the next guides.

---

## Section 1 - The Problem

### Choosing a Career is Harder Than Choosing a Degree

Topics:

- Most students know their degree but not their career options.
- Many graduates apply for jobs simply because everyone else is applying.
- Choosing a career should be an exploration exercise, not a guessing exercise.

---

## Section 2 - Your Mission

### Your 45-Minute Career Exploration Challenge

Mission:

At the end of the next 45 minutes, you will:

- Discover careers you may have never heard of.
- Shortlist careers that interest you.
- Understand what professionals in those careers do.
- Build your own Career Exploration Sheet.

---

## Section 3 - Start With Yourself

Ask yourself:

1. What are you studying?
2. What subjects do you enjoy?
3. What kind of problems do you enjoy solving?
4. What kind of people do you enjoy working with?
5. What kind of work sounds exciting to you?

Categories:

- Technology
- Business
- Finance
- Healthcare
- Design
- Education
- Operations
- Data
- Marketing
- Entrepreneurship
- Creativity
- Research

This section should take less than five minutes.

---

## Section 4 - Discover Careers Using AI

Mission:

> Discover at least 10 careers.

Teach students to ask questions such as:

```
I am pursuing a B.Com degree.

I enjoy teaching people and solving problems.

Suggest 20 career options that match my interests and explain what each professional does on a daily basis.
```

```
I enjoy working with people and technology.

Suggest careers that combine both.
```

```
Suggest careers that are expected to grow over the next 10 years.
```

Notice that we are teaching them how to think, not giving them magical prompts.

---

## Section 5 - Shortlist Your Careers

Students should shortlist:

```
Top 10 careers
↓
Top 5 careers
↓
Top 3 careers
```

Ask them:

- Which career sounds exciting?
- Which career aligns with your strengths?
- Which career offers growth opportunities?
- Which career would you enjoy learning more about?

---

## Section 6 - Research Your Top Three Careers

Students should use AI to answer questions like:

- What does a typical day look like?
- What are the entry-level roles?
- What skills are required?
- What tools do professionals use?
- What industries hire for this role?
- What does career progression look like?
- What are the advantages and challenges of this career?

This section is where AI becomes a career mentor rather than a search engine.

---

## Section 7 - Create Your Career Exploration Sheet

Students should create something like:

```
Career 1

Name:

Why I am interested in it:

Typical responsibilities:

Required skills:

Salary range:

Industries hiring:

Career progression:

What excites me about this career?

-------------------------------------------------

Career 2

-------------------------------------------------

Career 3
```

The student should leave this guide with something tangible.

---

## Section 8 - Things AI Cannot Tell You

Teach students that:

- AI cannot decide your career.
- AI cannot predict whether you will enjoy a job.
- AI cannot replace internships, conversations with professionals, or real experience.
- AI can help you think more clearly and make better decisions.

This section is important because it encourages healthy skepticism and responsible use of AI.

## Editorial Philosophy

We consciously avoid AI hype throughout this guide. AI is not positioned as a magic solution. Instead, AI should feel like an intelligent mentor sitting beside the student and helping them ask better questions.

If Communication Skills teaches students how employers think, this guide teaches students how to think about their careers.
