# What is Communication Competency?

---

## Purpose

Define what "communication competency" actually means in a professional context — separate from English language proficiency or basic conversation ability.

## Who It's For

- Students wondering if "communication skills" means just speaking English well
- Job seekers trying to understand what employers mean when they say "we need good communicators"
- Career explorers evaluating fit for customer-facing or leadership roles

## What It Is

**Communication competency is the ability to exchange information, ideas, and feedback effectively with others in professional settings — in a way that creates understanding, builds trust, and drives action.**

It has four dimensions:

### 1. **Clarity**
Expressing your thoughts in a way that's easy to understand. No jargon, no confusion, no ambiguity.

*Example:* Explaining to a customer why their request will take an extra 2 days — clearly, without excuses or technical mumbo-jumbo.

### 2. **Empathy**
Understanding what the other person thinks and feels, and adjusting your message accordingly.

*Example:* When a customer is frustrated, acknowledging their frustration first before explaining the solution, rather than jumping straight to facts.

### 3. **Engagement**
Keeping the other person's attention and interest. Not just talking AT them, but talking WITH them.

*Example:* Asking questions, listening to their response, and adapting based on what you hear — not delivering a prepared script.

### 4. **Consistency**
Aligning your words, tone, and body language. Saying one thing but looking or sounding like you mean something else destroys trust.

*Example:* Saying "I'm happy to help" with genuine tone and posture, not sighing and saying it with annoyance.

---

> **How this fits with the "five competencies":** these four dimensions describe what competency *is*. The five competencies employers assess — active listening, clarity, emotional intelligence, persuasion, adaptability — are how these dimensions show up at work. Two lenses on one thing, not two competing lists.

---

## Why This Matters

Communication competency is **not** the same as:

- ❌ **English language proficiency** — You can speak perfect English and still be unclear or confusing
- ❌ **Being talkative** — Some of the best communicators are quiet; they listen more than they speak
- ❌ **Presentation skills** — Communication happens in one-on-one conversations, team meetings, and emails, not just presentations
- ❌ **Charm or personality** — It's not about being charming; it's about being understood and building trust

**What it IS:** A learnable skill. You can get better at this through practice, feedback, and deliberate effort.

---

## Outcome

After reading this card, you understand that:

- Communication competency is about creating understanding and trust, not just speaking well
- It has measurable dimensions (clarity, empathy, engagement, consistency)
- It's a skill you can develop, not an innate talent
- It applies to all jobs, but is especially valued in roles involving customers, teams, or decisions

---

## Real-World Impact

A customer service representative with high communication competency:
- Resolves customer issues faster (fewer follow-up calls)
- Creates positive customer experiences (higher satisfaction scores)
- Earns faster promotions (leads know they can rely on them)
- Gets hired by better companies (is valued as a proven performer)
- Is consistently rewarded in pay and promotion — how large that reward is, and why, is covered in the salary-premium card
