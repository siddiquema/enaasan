# Checklist Standard

What makes a Checklist (see `templates/checklist-template.md`) complete and well-formed, on top of `publication-standard.md`.

## Requirements

- [ ] Every item is phrased as a concrete, checkable action, not a topic label — "Resume tailored to this specific job posting" (checkable) rather than "Resume" (not checkable, just a category).
- [ ] Items are ordered in the sequence they'd realistically be done, where an order exists — not alphabetically or arbitrarily.
- [ ] Any item that genuinely needs more than one line of explanation is a sign it belongs in a linked Guide instead — the Notes section may point to that Guide, but shouldn't become a second document embedded inside the checklist.
- [ ] The checklist is usable in one sitting, matching its `estimated_completion_time` — if completing it realistically takes multiple sessions across days, consider whether it's actually a Workbook instead (see `templates/workbook-template.md`).
- [ ] Grouped sections (if used) reflect genuinely distinct phases or categories, not an arbitrary split of a list that would work fine flat.

## What a Checklist is not

- Not a place for narrative explanation or persuasion — that's a Guide's job. A Checklist assumes the reader already understands *why* and just needs the *what* and *in what order*.
- Not a Workbook — no multi-stage tracker, no week-by-week structure. If it needs that, it's the wrong template.
