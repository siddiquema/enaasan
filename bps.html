BPS / KPO / BPO Careers

India's largest employed industry.

Over 5 million people work in Business Process Services in India. It is one of the most misunderstood career sectors — and one of the most accessible. Here is what it actually looks like from the inside.

Let us clear up the terminology first. BPO (Business Process Outsourcing), BPS (Business Process Services), and KPO (Knowledge Process Outsourcing) are often used interchangeably — but they describe different types of work.

BPO / BPS covers a wide range — from voice-based customer support to complex back-office operations in finance, HR, supply chain, and healthcare. The word "call centre" is an outdated and incomplete description of what this industry does today.

KPO is the knowledge-intensive end — legal process outsourcing, financial research, data analytics, market research, and clinical research. KPO roles typically require graduate or postgraduate qualifications and pay significantly more than traditional BPS.

The honest picture: BPS is not a fallback. It is a structured industry with clear entry points, defined growth paths, and salaries that are competitive with many other sectors for the same qualification level. The stigma around it is partly a perception problem — and partly the fact that not all companies within BPS are equal.

The full picture

BPS is not one thing — it is an entire spectrum

From answering customer calls to running complex legal research — here is the full range of what this industry covers.

📞
BPS — Voice
Customer Support & Tele-operations
Inbound and outbound customer service, technical support, collections, and tele-sales. The largest segment by headcount. Works in shifts including nights (for international clients).
Customer Service Rep Technical Support Collections Agent Tele-sales Executive
Entry: Class 12 pass, good English communication. Some roles accept 10th pass for domestic process.
₹1.8–4 LPA
💬
BPS — Non-Voice
Back Office & Transaction Processing
Data entry, document processing, claims handling, order management, account maintenance, email and chat support. Growing rapidly as companies move to digital interactions.
Data Entry Operator Back Office Executive Claims Processor Chat Support Agent
Entry: Class 12 or graduate. Typing speed and accuracy matter. English proficiency less critical than voice roles.
₹1.8–3.5 LPA
📊
F&A BPS
Finance & Accounting Operations
Accounts payable and receivable, reconciliations, financial reporting, payroll processing, and audit support. Operates as a shared service for large multinational companies. Requires accounting knowledge.
AP / AR Executive GL Accountant Payroll Analyst Finance Analyst RTR Analyst
Entry: B.Com / BBA / CA Inter. ERP knowledge (SAP, Oracle) is a strong differentiator.
₹2.5–7 LPA
👥
HR BPS
Human Resources Shared Services
HR operations outsourced by large companies — hiring support, onboarding, employee data management, payroll processing, HR helpdesk, and benefits administration.
HR Operations Executive Recruitment Coordinator Payroll Executive HR Analyst
Entry: Graduate (any stream). MBA-HR or PGDM for analyst roles. Communication and attention to detail are critical.
₹2.5–6 LPA
🔬
KPO — Analytics
Data Analytics & Research
Market research, business intelligence, data analysis, equity research, and consumer insights. Requires graduate-level analytical skills. One of the fastest-growing and best-paying BPS segments.
Research Analyst Data Analyst Equity Research Associate BI Analyst Insights Executive
Entry: Graduate (Statistics, Economics, Maths, Engineering). SQL and Excel essential. Python increasingly expected.
₹3.5–12 LPA
⚖️
KPO — LPO
Legal Process Outsourcing
Legal research, contract review and management, due diligence, intellectual property support, compliance monitoring, and litigation support. Serves international law firms and MNCs.
Legal Analyst Contract Reviewer IP Analyst Paralegal Compliance Analyst
Entry: LLB or BA LLB mandatory for most roles. NLU graduates at premium. US or UK law knowledge is an advantage.
₹4–15 LPA
🏥
KPO — Healthcare
Healthcare BPS & Medical Coding
Medical billing, coding (ICD-10, CPT), revenue cycle management, clinical data abstraction, and healthcare analytics. Primarily serves US healthcare providers.
Medical Coder Revenue Cycle Analyst Clinical Data Analyst Medical Transcriptionist
Entry: Life sciences graduate or paramedical background. CPC or CCS certification (AAPC) significantly improves prospects and salary.
₹2.5–8 LPA
💻
Tech-enabled BPS
Technology & AI-augmented Operations
The newest and fastest-growing segment. AI training and annotation, automation testing, digital content moderation, trust and safety operations, and intelligent document processing.
AI Trainer / Annotator Content Moderator Automation Analyst Digital Ops Specialist
Entry: Graduate any stream. Domain expertise (law, medicine, language) commands premium pay for specialised annotation roles.
₹2.5–10 LPA
ℹ️ *Salary ranges are indicative estimates based on publicly reported data from Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, NASSCOM BPS reports, and LinkedIn Salary Insights (2024–25). Night shift / international process roles typically pay 15–30% more than domestic process roles at the same level. Always verify with current job postings.

What nobody tells you

Navigating a BPS career — honestly.

01
Not all BPS companies are equal — research before you join
A role at Genpact, WNS, EXL, or Infosys BPM is a different experience from a small local BPO. Brand-name BPS companies offer structured training, clear growth paths, and better credentials on your resume. The extra effort to get into a tier-1 BPS company is worth it early in your career.
02
Night shifts are financially significant — but plan for them
International process roles (US/UK/Australia shift timings) pay 15–30% more than domestic process. For a fresher, this can mean ₹40,000–60,000 per month vs ₹20,000–25,000. If you are in your early career, international shift experience for 2–3 years can give you a meaningful financial head start and significantly stronger exposure to global processes.
03
The attrition trap — why people stay stuck
BPS has notoriously high attrition. Many people move laterally between companies for small salary bumps rather than growing vertically within one. Staying 2–3 years in one company and getting promoted is significantly more valuable for your career than job-hopping every 6 months for ₹2,000 more per month.
04
Build domain expertise — generalists plateau faster
A generalist voice agent can be replaced easily. An F&A analyst with SAP knowledge and 4 years of RTR experience is hard to find and well compensated. Pick a domain — finance, healthcare, legal, analytics — and go deep in it. Domain expertise is the single most effective way to increase your value and salary in BPS.
05
Certifications matter more in BPS than in most sectors
In BPS, a certification is often the difference between a ₹3 LPA and a ₹6 LPA role for the same experience level. COPC, Six Sigma, SAP, CPC (for healthcare), CAMS (for financial crimes compliance) — these are not optional extras. They are often the credentials that unlock the next level.
06
The MBA question — when it makes sense and when it does not
An MBA accelerates your move into management. But an MBA from a low-ranked college while staying in the same BPS company is often a significant financial cost for minimal career benefit. If you are going for an MBA, target a PGDM from a NAAC-A+ institution or better. Otherwise, domain certifications and internal promotions may give a better return.
07
AI is changing BPS — get ahead of it, not behind it
Automation and AI are reshaping transactional BPS roles — data entry, basic claims processing, and simple customer queries. The roles that are growing are the ones that require judgment, domain knowledge, and the ability to work with AI tools. Build AI literacy alongside your domain skills. The BPS professional who can manage AI-augmented workflows is the one companies will pay to retain.
08
Your communication skills are your most portable asset
BPS gives you something most desk jobs do not — intensive, daily practice communicating with clients, customers, and colleagues under pressure. The professionals who leave BPS and succeed in corporate roles consistently cite this as their biggest advantage. Treat every difficult customer interaction as communication training. It is.
