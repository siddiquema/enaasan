Privacy & Transparency

How we handle your information

enaasan is built for students. We take privacy seriously — especially because our audience includes minors.

What information we collect

enaasan does not require you to create an account or submit any personal information to use the site.

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors use the site — which pages are visited, how long people stay, and what devices are used. GA4 collects anonymised data including:

  • Pages visited and time spent
  • General geographic region (country/state level — not precise location)
  • Device type, browser, and operating system
  • Referral source (how you arrived at enaasan)

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any information that identifies you personally.

Cookies and local storage

enaasan uses browser local storage (not cookies) to remember your language preference across sessions. This stores only one value: your selected language (English, Tamil, Telugu, or Hindi). No personal data is stored.

Google Analytics sets cookies on your device to track anonymous session data. These cookies are governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

You can clear local storage and cookies at any time through your browser settings.

Analytics disclosure

enaasan uses Google Analytics 4, operated by Google LLC. By visiting this site, anonymous usage data is sent to Google's servers. Google may process this data in accordance with its own privacy policy, available at policies.google.com/privacy.

We use this data solely to understand how the site is being used so we can improve it. We do not sell, share, or monetise this data.

If you prefer not to be tracked by Google Analytics, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on available at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Third-party services

enaasan loads fonts from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com). Google Fonts may log the fonts requested and your IP address. See Google's privacy policy for details.

No other third-party services, advertising networks, or tracking pixels are used.
